So, where to buy games?

Here is a table of 4 online stores I recommend and use:

  1. Steam
  2. Epic Games Store
  3. Humble Bundle
  4. GOG.com

Steam

Steam is one of the largest and most popular platforms for PC gaming developed by Valve. It offers a vast library of games.
I enjoy their services like social networking, easy multiplayer gaming and lastly, support user-generated content and mods.
Every summer and winter, and during other special occasions, there are big sales, where everyone can get their games for a fair price.

Epic Games Store

Epic Games Store is the biggest competetor for Steam. Lately it's been getting exclusive game relases in order to attract people from Steam.
All in all it is similar to Steam, but it lacks Steam Workshop (a place for community made mods) and Community places. However, the
biggest difference is that Epic gives away free games every week! They are mostly small games, but during Christmas they give away big titles.

Humble Bundle

Humble Bundle offers a wide range of video games, software, and digital content at discounted prices. I enjoy their well-known pay-what-you-want
bundles, where you can choose how much to pay and give a portion of your purchase to charities. Simply said, you can legaly get cheaply games
and help some charity! Humble Bundle also operates a subscription service called Humble Choice, providing access to a selection of games
each month for a subscription fee. However, you can only buy retail keys there.

GOG.com

CD Projekt red created GOG to distribute old games, which weren't sold on Steam or other stores. Hence their original name-GOG.
It is a good choice if you want to try out retro games, but nowadays they offers new titles. They have developed GOG Galaxy
a game launcher where you can login through different game stores and launch/download games, without need for multiple launchers.
